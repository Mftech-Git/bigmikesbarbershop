The Man Behind the Chair

Meet Big Mike

Mike Carbajal runs the shop. One barber, one chair, no upsells, no gimmicks. If he’s not behind the chair, he’s with his family. That’s the whole story, and it’s the right one.

This is the kind of place barbershops used to be. Coffee on the counter, water in the cooler, and an honest cut for honest money.

Big Mike took over a tidy little spot on East Broadway in the Poets Square neighborhood and turned it into a real men’s shop. Walk in and you’ll find polished chrome, a striped pole out front, and a guy who actually cares whether your fade is clean and your line is sharp.

Customers come in for $25 cuts and leave talking about how much they laughed. Mike has a big laugh. He’s a husband and a father first, a barber second, and somehow he makes time for both. People say he treats every chair like it’s family in it. That’s not marketing. Read the reviews.

No appointments required. Walk-ins are the whole point. Pull up a chair, grab a coffee, and tell him how you want it.
